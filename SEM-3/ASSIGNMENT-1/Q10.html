S = vᵢt + ½at²: The Equation of Linear Motion

The formula S = vᵢt + ½at² describes the motion of an object under constant acceleration. Here:

  • S represents the displacement,
  • vi is the initial velocity,
  • t is the time,
  • a is the acceleration, and
  • 1/2 at2 is the additional displacement due to acceleration.

As stated, "An object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force."
